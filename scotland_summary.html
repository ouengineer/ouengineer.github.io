1
Saturday 18 April
Edinburgh → Glencoe
Drive 2h 45m ~3h 45m with stops
Rather than wasting the arrival evening in Edinburgh, the route heads immediately north on the A82 through Loch Lomond and Rannoch Moor — one of Britain's most dramatic roads — arriving in the Glencoe valley as the evening light turns the Three Sisters amber. Dinner and 300+ single malts await at the legendary Clachaig Inn, a stone's throw from where you'll sleep.

Sunday 19 April
Glencoe → Colbost, Skye
Drive 4h 15m ~7h elapsed w/ stops
Scotland's greatest one-day drive: Glenfinnan Viaduct at 9:45am before the coaches arrive, a pub lunch in Dornie before visiting Eilean Donan Castle, across the Skye Bridge and through to Colbost in the west for dinner at Three Chimneys — one of Scotland's most celebrated restaurants, 60 seconds from your hotel room. The day is a crescendo, every hour more beautiful than the last.

Monday 20 April
Colbost → Kinloch Lodge, Sleat
Drive ~2h ~3h 30m elapsed
The wildlife day. Dawn at Neist Point for puffins on their arrival week from the Atlantic, a tour of Talisker Distillery followed by a walk to the secret black-sand beach, a stop at Torabhaig — Skye's hidden second distillery — and an otter watch at dusk along the Isle Ornsay harbour before dinner at the Macdonalds' own estate lodge.

Tuesday 21 April
Skye → Oban → Gleneagles
Drive ~4h ~5h 30m elapsed
The transition day: from island wildness to grand hotel luxury, via the coastal A828 route — one of Scotland's most scenic roads — and a stop at the impossible Castle Stalker before arriving in Oban for whole West Coast langoustines at EE-USK. Arriving at Gleneagles in late afternoon feels like stepping from one world into another.

Wednesday 22 April
Gleneagles — Full Day
Drive 0 1h if Drummond Castle
A day of complete decompression: morning in The Retreat spa with a couple's Highland Journey treatment, an afternoon excursion to the extraordinary Drummond Castle Gardens (20 min away, almost no one goes), and the evening entirely devoted to Restaurant Andrew Fairlie — two Michelin stars, no windows, consistently ranked one of the UK's greatest restaurants. The trip's culinary centrepiece.

Thursday 23 April
Gleneagles → Edinburgh
Drive 1h 25m ~2h elapsed
Arriving in Edinburgh with five days of Scotland already lived, the city hits completely differently — unhurried oysters at the Café Royal, the afternoon on foot through Victoria Street and the Old Town, and an unmissable session at The Bow Bar (400 single malts, pub prices, extraordinary staff) before dinner at Dishoom or the Michelin-starred Number One, directly below your room at The Balmoral.

Friday 24 April
Edinburgh → Home
Airport 25 min Depart 4:30pm · Flight 7pm
